Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

How do annual members and casual riders use a bike-share service differently — and what does that mean for converting casual riders into members?

For this project, I analyzed a full year (2019) of Chicago bike-share trip data — roughly 3.8 million individual rides — to help a fictional company's marketing team understand rider behavior ahead of a membership conversion campaign. Working in Excel and Power Query, I cleaned and standardized four quarterly datasets (including handling a 1.6-million-row file that exceeded Excel's standard row limit), then analyzed ride patterns by rider type, day of week, and season. The data revealed a clear, consistent story: casual riders take significantly longer trips and ride mostly on weekends, while members ride short and steady throughout the work week — a seasonal, leisure-driven pattern versus a year-round commuter one. Based on these findings, I developed three data-backed marketing recommendations for converting casual riders into members.
